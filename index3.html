About
Our
Brand

Over 60 years ago, a
milk truck driver in
northern New York
complained to Julius Sämann about the
smell of spilled milk.
Sämann had lived and
worked in the
Canadian pine forests
researching the
extraction of aromatic
oils from pine needles.
He went to work and,
using exceptional
fragrances on
specialty material,
soon invented the first
automotive air freshener

The brand was
invented in Watertown,
New York in 1952 by
Julius Sämann, who
spent five cold years
extracting pine needle
oils in the Canadian
wilderness.

Wunder-baum Today

Today, in Europe Wunder-Baum is associated with raggare culture. Found mostly in Sweden and parts of Norway, Finland, Denmark, Germany and Austria, Raggare are known for their love of hot rod cars and 1950s American pop-culture.
